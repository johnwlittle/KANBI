About KANBI

KANBI is a lightweight, no-bullshit Kanban board application designed for simplicity and efficiency. It helps you visualize your workflow, limit work-in-progress, and maximize productivity without the bloat of complex project management tools.

Getting Started

Getting up and running with KANBI is quick and straightforward:

  1. Clone or download this repository. No dependencies or server required.
  2. Open index.html in your web browser
  3. For the best experience, disable your browser's download confirmation popups:
    • Chrome: Go to Settings → Downloads and turn off "Ask where to save each file before downloading"
    • Firefox: Go to Settings/Preferences → General → Files and Applications and uncheck "Always ask you where to save files"
    • Edge: Go to Settings → Downloads and turn off "Ask me what to do with each download"
    • Safari: Go to Safari → Preferences → General and uncheck "Show downloads window when downloading files"
  4. Start organizing your tasks!
  • KANBI saves your data automatically to your browser's local storage.
  • The "Export" button lets you save a backup of your board as a JSON file.
  • Start by creating a few tasks in the default columns, or customize your columns to match your workflow.

Core Philosophy

KANBI's ethos is centered around minimalism and practicality. We believe productivity tools should get out of your way and let you focus on what matters. KANBI is designed to be straightforward, fast, and reliable.

The application adheres to these core principles:

  • Simplicity: No unnecessary features or complex configurations
  • Speed: Optimized for performance, even on older devices
  • Privacy: Your data stays on your device with local storage
  • Flexibility: Customize your workflow without constraints
  • Portability: Export and import your boards easily

How to Use KANBI

Managing Columns

  • Add a Column: Click the "+ Column" button in the header or the "Add Column" box at the end of your board
  • Edit a Column: Click the pencil icon (✏️) on a column to edit its title or color
  • Reorder Columns: Drag columns horizontally to change their order
  • Delete a Column: Click the trash icon (🗑️) on a column (this will delete all tasks in that column)

Managing Tasks

  • Add a Task: Click the "+ Task" button in the header
  • Edit a Task: Click the pencil icon (✏️) on a task
  • Move a Task: Drag tasks between columns to change their status
  • Prioritize Tasks: Use the up/down arrows to reorder tasks within a column
  • Delete a Task: Click the trash icon (🗑️) on a task

Setting Due Dates

When creating or editing a task, you can set a due date. Tasks will display differently based on their due date status:

  • Normal: Due dates in the future
  • Due Today: Tasks due on the current day will show "⏰ Due Today" in amber
  • Overdue: Tasks past their due date will show "⚠️ Due: [date]" in red

Saving Your Work

  • Automatic Saving: KANBI automatically saves your board to your browser's local storage (usually the "Downloads" directory
  • Manual Export: Click the "Export" button to save your board as a JSON file
  • Importing: Click the "Import" button to load a previously exported board

Limitations & Workarounds

Single Board Limitation

KANBI currently supports one board per browser. For multiple boards, simply clone the KANBI index.html file into different directories and use them as separate instances.

File Management

Exporting creates new JSON files. Periodically clean up your downloads folder to avoid clutter. You may want to create a dedicated folder for your KANBI board exports.

Local Storage

Your data is stored in your browser. Clearing browser data will erase your board. Export regularly to avoid data loss. When using multiple browsers or devices, manually export/import to keep your boards in sync.

Task Limits

While there's no hard limit on tasks, performance may degrade with hundreds of tasks. Consider archiving completed work regularly by exporting your board, clearing finished tasks, and saving the archive file for reference.

Tips & Tricks

  • Use consistent column colors for better visual organization. For example, use cooler colors for early stages and warmer colors for completion stages.
  • Export your board regularly as a backup, especially before making significant changes.
  • Keep task descriptions concise for better readability and overall board performance.
  • Use due dates strategically to highlight time-sensitive tasks rather than assigning them to everything.
  • Limit work-in-progress by keeping task counts in middle columns low. This improves focus and completion rates.
  • Create a workflow that matches your process, not the other way around. Add, remove, or rename columns to fit your needs.
  • Review and clean up your board periodically to maintain performance and focus on current priorities.
